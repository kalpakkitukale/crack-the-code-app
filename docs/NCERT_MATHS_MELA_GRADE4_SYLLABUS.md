## Chapter 1 — Shapes Around Us

**Key Concepts:** 2D vs 3D shapes, faces/edges/vertices, shape identification in real life

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| 2D Shapes | Circles, triangles, squares, rectangles, hexagons — sides, corners |
| 3D Shapes | Cube, cuboid, sphere, cylinder, cone, pyramid — faces, edges, vertices |
| Faces of 3D shapes | Identifying which 2D shapes form the faces of 3D objects |
| Real-life shapes | Finding shapes in everyday objects around the house/school |

**Activities:** Rolling vs stacking shapes, making shapes from clay, tracing faces of 3D objects to get 2D shapes.

---

## Chapter 2 — Hide and Seek

**Key Concepts:** Spatial reasoning, position, direction, 3D views, top/front/side views

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Position & direction | Left, right, above, below, between, near, far |
| Map reading | Reading simple maps, understanding directions |
| Paths and routes | Following and describing routes on a grid |
| Top/front/side views | Identifying different views of 3D objects |

**Activities:** Drawing a map of classroom/school, following routes on a grid, looking at objects from different angles.

---

## Chapter 3 — Patterns Around Us

**Key Concepts:** Number patterns, shape patterns, repeating and growing patterns

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Repeating patterns | Identifying and extending colour/shape repeating patterns |
| Growing patterns | Number sequences that increase by a fixed amount |
| Number patterns | Odd/even, multiples, patterns in addition tables |
| Patterns in nature | Patterns on leaves, flowers, tiles, cloth |

**Activities:** Creating patterns with stamps, tiling patterns, finding patterns in a hundreds chart, rangoli-inspired patterns.

---

## Chapter 4 — Thousands Around Us

**Key Concepts:** Place value up to 5 digits, number names, expanded form, comparison

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Place value | Ones, tens, hundreds, thousands, ten-thousands |
| Reading & writing numbers | Number names in words (Indian number system) |
| Expanded form | Breaking numbers: 45,328 = 40,000 + 5,000 + 300 + 20 + 8 |
| Comparing numbers | Using >, <, = for 4- and 5-digit numbers |
| Ordering numbers | Ascending and descending order |
| Estimation & rounding | Rounding to nearest 10, 100, 1000 |

**Activities:** Abacus activities, representing numbers on spike abacus, reading distances/populations from maps.

---

## Chapter 5 — Sharing and Measuring

**Key Concepts:** Fractions as equal parts, unit fractions, comparing fractions, fractions of a collection

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Equal parts | Dividing shapes/objects into equal parts |
| Unit fractions | ½, ⅓, ¼, ⅙, ⅛ — one part of the whole |
| Non-unit fractions | ¾, ⅔, ⅝ — multiple parts of the whole |
| Fractions of a collection | Finding ½ of 12 mangoes, ¼ of 20 marbles |
| Comparing fractions | Which is bigger: ½ or ¼? Using diagrams |
| Fractions on number line | Placing ½, ¼, ¾ on a number line |

**Activities:** Sharing dhokla equally, folding paper into equal parts, shading fraction strips, distributing toffees among friends.

---

## Chapter 6 — Measuring Length

**Key Concepts:** Standard units (mm, cm, m, km), conversions, measuring and estimating

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Units of length | Millimetres, centimetres, metres, kilometres |
| Conversions | 10 mm = 1 cm, 100 cm = 1 m, 1000 m = 1 km |
| Measuring with ruler | Reading measurements accurately |
| Estimating length | Guessing lengths before measuring |
| Adding & subtracting lengths | Word problems with mixed units |
| Perimeter | Distance around a shape (sum of all sides) |

**Activities:** Measuring classroom objects, body measurements (handspan, arm length), measuring a running track.

## Chapter 7 — The Cleanest Village

**Key Concepts:** Multi-digit addition and subtraction, money, word problems in context

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Multi-digit addition | Adding 3- and 4-digit numbers with regrouping |
| Multi-digit subtraction | Subtracting 3- and 4-digit numbers with borrowing |
| Money | Indian currency — paise, rupees; making change |
| Magic triangles | Puzzle: placing numbers so each side sums to the same total |
| Word problems | Real-life problems involving addition/subtraction and money |

**Context:** Story set in Mawlynnong village (Asia's cleanest village), collecting money for community projects, budgeting.

**Activities:** Magic triangle puzzles, shopping simulations, calculating totals and change.

## Chapter 8 — Weigh it, Pour it

**Key Concepts:** Mass (grams, kilograms), capacity (millilitres, litres), measuring and estimating

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Units of mass | Grams (g) and kilograms (kg); 1 kg = 1000 g |
| Weighing | Using balance scale, reading weighing scale |
| Units of capacity | Millilitres (ml) and litres (L); 1 L = 1000 ml |
| Measuring capacity | Reading measuring jugs and cups |
| Estimation | Estimating weight and capacity before measuring |
| Word problems | Problems involving mass and capacity |

**Activities:** Weighing classroom objects, measuring water into containers, comparing weights of vegetables at a market.

---

## Chapter 9 — Equal Groups

**Key Concepts:** Multiplication as repeated addition, division as equal sharing/grouping, multiplication tables

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Multiplication concept | Equal groups → repeated addition → multiplication |
| Multiplication tables | Tables 1–10; patterns in tables |
| Properties of multiplication | Commutative (3×4 = 4×3), zero, one |
| Division concept | Equal sharing and equal grouping |
| Relation between × and ÷ | If 3×4=12 then 12÷3=4 and 12÷4=3 |
| Division with remainder | 17 ÷ 3 = 5 remainder 2 |

**Activities:** Arranging objects in arrays, packing items into equal groups, word problems about dividing biscuits/sweets.

---

## Chapter 10 — Elephants, Tigers, and Leopards

**Key Concepts:** Large number addition/subtraction, reading data tables, NIM game, Reverse-and-Add

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Reading data tables | Population tables of wildlife across Indian states |
| 4-digit addition | Adding large numbers from wildlife census data |
| 4-digit subtraction | Finding differences (how many more elephants in Karnataka vs Kerala?) |
| NIM game | Strategic addition game: add 1 or 2, first to reach 10 wins |
| Reverse-and-Add | Take a number, reverse digits, add — explore patterns |
| Pattern charts | Addition tables with sums, identifying diagonal patterns |

**Context:** Real NCERT wildlife data: elephants (Karnataka 6049, Kerala 5706, Assam 5719...), tigers, leopards across states.

**Activities:** NIM game strategy, Reverse-and-Add chains, completing addition grids, wildlife data word problems.

---

## Chapter 11 — Fun with Symmetry

**Key Concepts:** Lines of symmetry, mirror images, completing symmetric figures

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Symmetry concept | A shape is symmetric if one half is a mirror of the other |
| Line of symmetry | The fold line that divides a shape into equal halves |
| Multiple lines of symmetry | Square (4), rectangle (2), circle (infinite), equilateral triangle (3) |
| Mirror images | Drawing reflections of shapes |
| Completing figures | Given half a shape, draw the other half |
| Symmetry in letters & nature | Symmetric letters (A, H, M), butterfly wings, leaf veins |

**Activities:** Paper folding to find symmetry, ink blot butterflies, completing half-drawn shapes on grid paper.

---

## Chapter 12 — Ticking Clocks and Turning Calendar

**Key Concepts:** Reading time (hours, minutes), AM/PM, 24-hour clock, calendar, elapsed time

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Reading analogue clock | Hours and minutes on a 12-hour clock |
| Reading digital clock | Digital time format (3:45, 11:30) |
| AM and PM | 12-hour vs 24-hour time |
| Time intervals | How many minutes between two times? |
| Calendar reading | Days, weeks, months, year |
| Elapsed time | How many days from 5th March to 20th April? |
| Scheduling | Reading timetables, train schedules |

**Activities:** Making daily schedule, reading train timetables, calculating age in days/weeks.

---

## Chapter 13 — The Transport Museum

**Key Concepts:** Multiplication tables 11–20, multiplying by multiples of 10, division with remainder

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Multiplication tables 11–20 | Tables of 11, 12, 13 … up to 20 |
| Multiplying by 10, 20, 50, 100 | 35 × 10 = 350; 24 × 20 = 480 |
| 2-digit × 1-digit | Standard algorithm and mental methods |
| Division with remainder | 47 ÷ 5 = 9 remainder 2 |
| Word problems | Problems set in a transport museum context |

**Context:** Story about visiting a transport museum — counting wheels of vehicles, ticket prices, distances between cities.

**Activities:** Calculating total wheels on buses/cycles, ticket cost calculations, skip counting on number line.

---

## Chapter 14 — Data Handling

**Key Concepts:** Collecting data, tally marks, pictographs, bar graphs, interpreting graphs

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Collecting & organising data | Survey, asking questions, recording answers |
| Tally marks | Counting method; 5th tally crosses the previous four |
| Frequency table | Organising tally marks into a table |
| Pictograph | Each symbol represents a fixed number; reading scales |
| Bar graph | Drawing and reading bar graphs with labelled axes |
| Interpreting graphs | Answering questions: most, least, total, difference |

**Activities:** Class surveys (favourite fruit, mode of transport), drawing bar graphs on grid paper, reading pictographs from newspapers.

## Assessment Notes

- Competency-based questions (not rote recall)
- Activity-based learning throughout — "do and discover" approach
- No separate theory chapters — concepts emerge from stories and activities
- Emphasis on mental math, estimation, and real-life problem solving
- Hindi and regional language contexts included in problems
